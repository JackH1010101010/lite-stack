# Opportunity Research and Portfolio Model v0

## Purpose

This document defines how the company researches opportunities, runs parallel tracks, promotes promising wedges, and manages a growing portfolio without losing clarity.

Its role is operational. The Operating Doctrine defines the permanent principles. The Working Venture Thesis defines the current business hypothesis. This document defines how opportunities move through the system.

The goal is to allow broad exploration without creating strategic sprawl, duplicated effort, or confusing market-facing execution.

## Core Rule

The company may explore many opportunities in parallel, but it must manage them as a bounded portfolio.

Parallelism is allowed when it improves learning speed, comparison quality, or opportunity discovery. Parallelism is not an excuse for running many vague businesses at once.

The portfolio exists to help the company discover, compare, select, deepen, and scale opportunities in a disciplined way.

## Units of Analysis

### Idea

An idea is a raw possibility. It may be a market, city, wedge, customer promise, channel, pricing angle, or operational insight. Ideas are cheap and numerous.

### Research Track

A research track is a structured inquiry into a plausible opportunity area. It may focus on a city, market family, user segment, positioning angle, or infrastructure path. A research track exists to turn loose ideas into comparable hypotheses.

### Hypothesis

A hypothesis is a testable claim about what may work. It should be specific enough to evaluate, such as:

* premium last-minute booking may outperform budget urgency in a given destination type
* one city may have better economics than another for the same wedge
* a specific customer promise may convert better than alternatives

### Experiment

An experiment is a bounded test designed to change a decision. It has a clear question, scope, time horizon, success threshold, and evaluation method.

### Wedge

A wedge is a focused business expression built around a narrow promise. It is more concrete than a hypothesis and more market-facing than an experiment. A wedge may combine:

* a market or destination pattern
* a customer segment
* a message
* a funnel model
* a booking or product behavior

### Live Wedge

A live wedge is a wedge that has moved beyond pure exploration and is receiving active market-facing execution. It may have its own pages, funnel, economics, and operating attention.

### Brand

A brand is a distinct market-facing identity. Not every wedge needs its own brand. A separate brand should exist only when it improves clarity, economics, or strategic separation.

### Portfolio Bet

A portfolio bet is a live wedge that the company has intentionally chosen to continue or scale. It has earned ongoing resources.

## Lifecycle

The default lifecycle is:

Idea -> Research Track -> Hypothesis -> Experiment -> Live Wedge -> Portfolio Bet -> Scaled Business or Brand

Not every opportunity will pass through every stage. Many should stop early.

### Stage 1: Idea

The purpose of this stage is generation. Ideas may come from research, customer behavior, search patterns, market data, operator intuition, or autonomous systems.

The standard for entry is low. The standard for promotion is not.

### Stage 2: Research Track

The purpose of this stage is structuring inquiry. A research track should define:

* what opportunity family is being explored
* what market, city, or segment is in scope
* what question is being asked
* what evidence would make the track more or less attractive

A track should result in comparable hypotheses, not just notes.

### Stage 3: Hypothesis

The purpose of this stage is to make the opportunity testable. A valid hypothesis should be:

* specific
* falsifiable
* decision-relevant
* comparable with alternatives

A hypothesis should identify what must be true for the wedge to deserve an experiment.

### Stage 4: Experiment

The purpose of this stage is to generate signal. An experiment should have:

* a named hypothesis
* a clear scope
* a fixed duration or review point
* a measurement method
* promotion, pause, or kill criteria

An experiment exists to reduce uncertainty, not to simulate progress.

### Stage 5: Live Wedge

The purpose of this stage is concentrated execution. A live wedge should have:

* one dominant customer promise
* one clear market-facing identity
* a defined target user or demand pattern
* active measurement of economics and conversion
* explicit owner attention

A live wedge may remain within an existing brand or become distinct enough to justify separation.

### Stage 6: Portfolio Bet

The purpose of this stage is intentional continuation or scaling. A portfolio bet has already earned evidence of viability and now justifies ongoing investment.

At this stage, the company should be clear about whether the wedge is:

* part of the main business
* a separated wedge under the same brand family
* a candidate for a new brand or operating unit

### Stage 7: Scaled Business or Brand

The purpose of this stage is compounding. A scaled business or brand is not simply an experiment that has lasted longer. It is an execution unit with repeatable economics, clear identity, and operational stability.

## Promotion Criteria

A track, experiment, or wedge should be promoted when the evidence is strong enough to justify more attention, capital, or separation.

Promotion should usually require a combination of:

* meaningful customer pain or urgency
* demand that appears large enough or valuable enough
* a customer promise that can be expressed clearly in one sentence
* attractive enough unit economics or credible path to them
* reachable distribution
* evidence that the wedge is differentiated from alternatives
* supply and operational viability
* stronger performance than relevant comparison tracks
* evidence that additional revenue does not require roughly matching increases in live human involvement, manual delivery, or operational oversight

Promotion is comparative, not absolute. The question is not only whether something is good. The question is whether it is better than the current alternatives.

A wedge should also be judged by the calendar-control test. If it only works when operators remain continuously present for manual coordination, delivery, approvals, or follow-up, then the economics are weaker than they first appear. Strong wedges should become more supportable by systems, assets, automation, and repeatable operating structures as they mature.

## Kill Criteria

A track, experiment, or wedge should be paused or killed when:

* the customer pain is weak or hard to observe
* the market is too small, too poor, or too inaccessible
* the message remains unclear after honest iteration
* economics are structurally unattractive
* the wedge cannot be separated from stronger alternatives
* a better opportunity clearly dominates it
* the effort required is too high relative to expected reward
* the opportunity creates complexity without corresponding strategic advantage
* each increment of growth appears to require matching increments of human attention, coordination, or operational strain

Killing is not failure. It is a necessary part of keeping the portfolio sharp.

## Separation Criteria

A wedge may stay within the main business, become a clearly separated wedge, or become a distinct brand.

### Keep within the same business when:

* the customer promise is closely related
* the audience overlap is high
* the funnel and operational model are similar
* shared brand trust improves conversion
* separation would add more complexity than clarity

### Separate into a distinct wedge when:

* the message needs more focus than the current brand can support
* the audience differs enough to require distinct positioning
* the funnel or economics differ materially
* comparison and management improve when the wedge is treated separately

### Consider a new brand when:

* the customer promise is truly distinct
* shared branding would create confusion or weaken trust
* the market position materially differs from the parent brand
* the wedge is viable enough to justify dedicated identity and attention
* the operating model can be kept separate without harmful overhead

The rule is not one business forever. The rule is clarity per execution unit.

## Portfolio Limits

The portfolio should remain bounded.

The purpose of limits is not bureaucracy. The purpose is to protect attention and preserve comparability.

As a default guideline:

* many ideas may exist at once
* a limited number of research tracks should be active at once
* fewer hypotheses should become active experiments
* only a manageable number of live wedges should receive concentrated market-facing execution at one time

The exact number may change by stage, team size, and system maturity. The principle is that active execution capacity is always narrower than research capacity.

### Early-Phase Default Limits

Until the company deliberately revises them, use the following as default operating caps:

- up to 8 active research tracks
- up to 4 active experiments
- up to 2 live wedges receiving concentrated market-facing execution
- more than 1 new brand incubation at a time requires explicit portfolio review and written justification

These are defaults, not immutable rules. Exceeding them should trigger a conscious review rather than happen implicitly.

## Evidence Standards

Different stages require different evidence.

### For moving from idea to research track

The standard is plausibility.

### For moving from research track to hypothesis

The standard is structured reasoning and enough evidence to define a real question.

### For moving from hypothesis to experiment

The standard is decision-relevance. The answer must matter.

### For moving from experiment to live wedge

The standard is real signal, not just interesting anecdotes.

### For moving from live wedge to portfolio bet

The standard is repeatable evidence across conversion, economics, message clarity, and operational feasibility.

Whenever possible, decisions should combine quantitative evidence, qualitative evidence, and comparative context.

## Cadence

The portfolio should be reviewed on a regular rhythm.

### Ongoing

Research tracks, experiments, and signals may update continuously.

### Weekly

Review active tracks and experiments. Decide what continues, what changes, and what stops.

### Monthly

Review portfolio shape. Decide whether any wedge should be promoted, separated, merged, or killed.

### Quarterly

Review whether the current portfolio still matches the doctrine and venture thesis. Reassess whether the company is becoming clearer, stronger, and more economically justified.

## Decision Rights

Until a more formal governance model exists, the default decision rights are as follows.

- ideas and research tracks may be opened by founders, team members, or approved autonomous systems
- promotion from research track to experiment requires explicit human approval
- promotion from experiment to live wedge requires founder or designated portfolio-owner approval
- promotion from live wedge to portfolio bet requires explicit portfolio review
- creation of a new brand or separate operating unit requires founder approval
- autonomous systems may recommend promotions, pauses, kills, and pruning actions, but they may not unilaterally approve new live wedges, new brands, or irreversible structural changes unless governance rules are explicitly updated later

The purpose of these rights is not bureaucracy. The purpose is to keep autonomy useful, bounded, and strategically aligned.

## Decision Logging

Every significant portfolio decision should be recorded.

This includes:

* what was decided
* what evidence supported the decision
* what alternatives were considered
* what assumptions remain uncertain
* what would cause reversal

A decision log improves learning and reduces repeated confusion.

## Role of Autonomous Systems

Autonomous systems may help with:

* market sensing
* opportunity generation
* scoring and comparison
* content and page experiments
* monitoring and reporting
* pruning low-value tracks

They should not be allowed to create uncontrolled portfolio sprawl. Their role is to improve learning speed and execution quality inside explicit portfolio rules.

## Success Standard

This model is working when:

* many possible opportunities are explored without creating chaos
* weak ideas die early
* strong wedges become clearer over time
* promotion decisions are evidence-based
* the number of live execution priorities stays manageable
* multiple winners can coexist when justified without blurring the company
* revenue and output can increase without proportional increases in human pressure or calendar dependence

The end state is not maximal variety. The end state is a disciplined portfolio of clear, economically justified opportunities.

## Summary

The company may run multiple opportunities, markets, locations, wedges, and experiments in parallel, but they must move through a structured portfolio model.

Research should remain broad. Promotion should be selective. Execution should stay clear. Scale should be earned. Separation should happen only when it improves clarity, economics, or strategic control.
