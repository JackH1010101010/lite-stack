## Wholesale Rate Estimation Methodology

### Industry Benchmarks Used

The existing pricing research (research/pricing/pricing-strategy-research.md) documents the following rate structure:

| Rate Type | Typical Discount vs Rack Rate | Source |
|-----------|------------------------------|--------|
| OTA Rate to Guest | 15-25% below rack | AltexSoft, industry standard |
| CUG/Member-Only Rates | 10-20% below rack | Industry standard |
| Wholesale/Bed Bank Net Rates | 20-40% below rack | AltexSoft, LiteAPI docs |

For this analysis, I've used a **conservative 25-30% discount below public OTA rates** as the wholesale estimate, based on:
1. OTA rates are already 15-25% below rack rate
2. Wholesale rates are 20-40% below rack rate
3. The spread between OTA rate and wholesale rate is therefore approximately 15-30%
4. LiteAPI provides net rates via their API with configurable markup parameters (margin + additionalMarkup)
5. Our platform applies a 15% markup on top of the wholesale rate

**Important caveat:** These are estimates. Actual LiteAPI wholesale rates will vary by property, date, and availability. The real savings should be validated by querying the LiteAPI rates endpoint for specific properties and comparing to live OTA rates.

### How to Validate

To confirm these estimates, run the following for each hero property:
1. Query LiteAPI `/hotels/rates` endpoint for a specific date range
2. Capture the net rate returned
3. Compare against the same-date rate on Booking.com/Expedia
4. Calculate actual spread

### Wholesale Rate Methodology — Refined (Cycle 2)

#### LiteAPI-Specific Insights

From LiteAPI's official documentation (docs.liteapi.travel):
- LiteAPI supports **configurable markup**: a default markup (e.g., 10%) plus an additional markup parameter. Our 15% markup aligns with their system.
- The **Suggested Selling Price (SSP)** is the hotel's recommended minimum selling price. Sellers can sell at or above SSP publicly, but **must not sell below SSP on public-facing platforms** — CUG gating satisfies this requirement.
- LiteAPI introduced **Dynamic Markup** in 2025: automatically adjusts pricing based on real-time market conditions. This means actual savings may vary dynamically rather than being a fixed percentage.

#### Industry Benchmark Validation

From Hotel Tech Report's 2025 bed bank analysis and AltexSoft:
- Wholesale/bed bank rates are typically **20-40% below rack rates** (confirmed from Cycle 1)
- OTAs charge **15-25% commission** on bookings (Booking.com 15-18%, Expedia 18-25%)
- Hotels offer better rates to trade/wholesale because agents bring volume and handle customer service without the 15-25% OTA commission overhead
- Wholesale rates even after bed bank markup are "still lower than published rates" (Hotel Tech Report)
- Non-major OTAs increasingly show lower prices than hotels' own rates via wholesaler reselling — this is the exact mechanism our CUG platform leverages legally through member-gating

**Key validation point:** The 15-30% savings estimate from Cycle 1 is consistent with industry data. The actual spread varies by property, season, and demand. High-demand periods (peak season Maldives Dec-Apr) may see narrower spreads as wholesale rates firm up. Low-season periods (June-August for most destinations) may see wider spreads.

### OTA Rate Parity Risks — What Could Undermine Our Wholesale Spread

Research from [Hotelogix](https://blog.hotelogix.com/hotels-about-rate-parity/), [Lighthouse (formerly OTA Insight)](https://www.mylighthouse.com/resources/blog/hotel-rate-parity-issues), and [PhocusWire](https://www.phocuswire.com/hotels-grapple-secondary-otas-pricing-control) reveals rate parity is a live and worsening issue in 2025-2026:

#### The Problem: Wholesale Rate Leakage

Hotels sell rooms to bed banks (like the suppliers behind LiteAPI) at wholesale rates, intending them for B2B redistribution within closed channels. However, these rates frequently "leak" to public-facing platforms — secondary OTAs, metasearch sites, and loosely-managed affiliates — who undercut the hotel's own direct rates.

**How this affects us:**

1. **Risk: Hotels crack down on wholesale distribution.** If Hilton (Waldorf Astoria), Marriott (St. Regis), or Soneva detect their wholesale rates appearing publicly at prices below their rate parity agreements, they may restrict wholesale access. Our CUG member-gating mitigates this — we're technically not public-facing — but we need to ensure our Google Sign-In wall genuinely qualifies as a CUG under hotel rate parity definitions.

2. **Risk: Secondary OTAs already offer similar rates.** Non-major OTAs like Agoda, Guest Reservations, and smaller booking sites are increasingly showing rates sourced from wholesale channels. If guests can find these rates without membership, our "exclusive rates" value prop weakens. Counter: These secondary OTAs lack brand trust and have poor user experiences. Our luxury positioning + trustworthy UX is the differentiator, not just price.

3. **Opportunity: EU rate parity regulation.** The EU required Booking Holdings to remove rate parity clauses across Europe in 2025. The UK, Australia, Japan, and South Korea have similar laws. This means hotels can now offer lower direct rates without OTA penalty — which could widen or narrow our spread depending on whether hotels pass savings to wholesale or direct channels.

#### Mitigation Strategies

- **Ensure CUG compliance is bulletproof:** The LiteAPI SSP (Suggested Selling Price) documentation explicitly permits selling below SSP in CUG channels. Our Google Sign-In requirement satisfies this, but we should document our CUG status in ToS and supplier communications.
- **Monitor rate parity actively:** Use a tool like Lighthouse or OTA Insight to track whether our platform rates are being undercut by secondary OTAs. If they are, our value prop shifts from price to convenience + trust.
- **Don't over-promise specific savings percentages.** Use ranges ("save 15-30%") rather than fixed numbers, since wholesale spreads fluctuate with demand, season, and rate parity enforcement.

### LiteAPI Wholesale Rate Model — Refined Understanding (Cycle 3)

Building on Cycle 2's LiteAPI documentation review, additional research confirms the wholesale rate model:

**From [LiteAPI Revenue Management docs](https://docs.liteapi.travel/docs/revenue-management-and-commission):**

- Net rates are provided by suppliers (hotel chains, bed banks, aggregators). LiteAPI aggregates across 2M+ properties.
- The `margin` parameter controls your markup. Setting margin=15 adds 15% to the net rate.
- The `additionalMarkup` parameter adds further on top of default margin. Example: 10% default + 15% additional = 25% total markup on net rate.
- **SSP (Suggested Selling Price)** is the hotel's minimum public rate. Selling below SSP is permitted ONLY in CUG/logged-in environments — exactly our model.

**From [Nuitée/LiteAPI 2025 Product Updates](https://nuitee.com/insights/lite-api-product-updates-2025):**

- Dynamic Markup (enterprise feature) automatically adjusts pricing based on competitor rates. This could mean our margin automatically widens when OTA rates spike (peak season) and narrows when they drop (off-season).
- The 2025 updates improved rate coverage and added more luxury inventory sources.

**Industry benchmark validation from [AltexSoft](https://www.altexsoft.com/blog/hotel-rates/):**

- Wholesale/bed bank rates are typically 20-40% below rack rate (confirmed across all 3 cycles).
- After bed bank markup and redistribution, the rates reaching end sellers are "still lower than published rates."
- Our 15% markup model sits well within the range that preserves meaningful savings vs OTA rates.

**Updated confidence assessment:** The 15-30% savings vs OTA estimate from Cycles 1-2 remains our best working model. The real spread will vary by property and date, but the structural economics — wholesale net rates + 15% markup vs OTA rates that include 15-25% OTA commission — consistently favour our platform for consumers.
