Maldives vs Seychelles honeymoon: which destination should you choose?

By LuxStay Research · Updated 13 March 2026 · 7 min read

The Maldives and Seychelles are the two most popular luxury honeymoon destinations in the Indian Ocean, and choosing between them is one of the most debated questions in luxury travel. Our analysis of 64 resorts across both destinations — 43 in the Maldives and 21 in the Seychelles — using aggregated pricing, guest review data, and proprietary quality scoring reveals meaningful differences that go beyond the usual travel blog platitudes.

The headline finding: the Maldives delivers a more exclusive, water-centric honeymoon experience with higher peak prices (water villas averaging £450–£2,500/night at member rates) but also more accommodation variety. The Seychelles offers a more adventurous, nature-rich experience with better value at the mid-luxury tier — average 5-star member rates are 25% lower than Maldives equivalents. For pure romance and overwater living, the Maldives wins. For couples who want beaches plus hiking, wildlife, and island-hopping, the Seychelles is the stronger choice.

Maldives: What to expect

The Maldives has defined the luxury honeymoon template for good reason. The nation's 1,200 islands — most hosting a single resort — create a level of privacy and seclusion impossible to replicate elsewhere. The one-island-one-resort model means your entire world for 5–7 nights is a coral island surrounded by a turquoise lagoon, with no roads, no traffic, and no outside interruptions.

For honeymoons specifically, the Maldives excels at curated romantic experiences. Most resorts offer dedicated honeymoon packages: private sandbank dinners, couples' spa treatments over the water, sunset dolphin cruises, and floating breakfast service. The overwater villa category (available at most 5-star properties) is purpose-built for the romantic market — glass floors, outdoor bathtubs, direct lagoon access at sunset.

The limitation is variety. After day 3–4, the 'island life' rhythm of beach, pool, snorkel, spa, dinner can feel repetitive for active couples. Excursions exist (fishing, diving, local island visits) but they're add-ons, not integrated into the destination. Our guest review analysis shows satisfaction scores dip slightly on stays longer than 7 nights — the Maldives is optimised for a 4–6 night honeymoon segment.

Seychelles: What to expect

The Seychelles offers a fundamentally different honeymoon proposition. The 115-island archipelago has three main inhabited islands (Mahé, Praslin, La Digue) plus dozens of private and semi-private resort islands. This creates an opportunity for island-hopping itineraries that combine beach luxury with genuine exploration — hiking through the Vallée de Mai (UNESCO World Heritage), encountering giant Aldabra tortoises, and discovering hidden beaches accessible only by boat.

The beaches themselves are visually distinct from the Maldives. Where Maldivian beaches are flat white sand meeting turquoise water, Seychelles beaches feature dramatic granite boulders rising from pink-tinged sand — Anse Source d'Argent on La Digue is routinely voted among the world's most beautiful. This geological drama gives the Seychelles a photographic distinctiveness that the Maldives' uniform beauty can't match.

The value proposition is compelling. Our data shows Seychelles 5-star resorts average 25% lower member rates than equivalent-quality Maldives properties. Part of this reflects the broader range of accommodation styles — boutique guesthouses, hillside villas, and plantation-style hotels sit alongside ultra-luxury resorts. For honeymooners willing to split their trip (3 nights luxury resort + 3 nights boutique island-hopping), the Seychelles can deliver a richer overall experience at a lower total cost.

Price comparison: Member rates vs OTA pricing

The pricing gap between Maldives and Seychelles luxury resorts is significant and consistent. At the 5-star level, Maldives member rates average £350–£2,500/night compared to £250–£1,200 in the Seychelles. The gap widens at the ultra-luxury tier: a Maldives overwater villa at a top-10 resort costs 50–80% more than a comparable Seychelles hillside villa with private pool.

However, the total trip cost comparison is more nuanced. Maldives resorts are typically all-inclusive or half-board (because there's nowhere else to eat), which bundles meals and drinks into the rate. Seychelles resorts more commonly offer room-only or B&B rates, with dining charged separately. When you factor in dining, the effective gap narrows to about 15–20%.

Member rate savings are stronger in the Seychelles (average 22% below OTA pricing) versus the Maldives (19%). For a 7-night honeymoon, this translates to a saving of £800–£2,000 in the Seychelles and £900–£3,000 in the Maldives — both substantial, but the Maldives savings are larger in absolute terms due to higher base prices.

The verdict

Both destinations deliver exceptional honeymoon experiences, but they suit different couple profiles. The data supports a clear framework for choosing.

Choose Maldives if you want:

  • You want the iconic overwater villa experience
  • Pure beach relaxation is your priority
  • You're comfortable in one resort for 5–7 nights
  • Snorkelling and marine life matter most
  • Budget isn't the primary constraint

Choose Seychelles if you want:

  • You want variety — beaches, hiking, wildlife, culture
  • Island-hopping appeals to you
  • Better value matters (25% lower at equivalent quality)
  • You prefer dramatic, photogenic landscapes (granite beaches)
  • You want more to do beyond the resort

Frequently asked questions

Which is cheaper for a luxury honeymoon — Maldives or Seychelles? ▼
The Seychelles is approximately 25% cheaper at the 5-star level. Our analysis shows average member rates of £250–£1,200/night for Seychelles luxury resorts compared to £350–£2,500/night in the Maldives. A 7-night honeymoon in the Seychelles typically costs £3,500–£10,000 at member rates versus £5,000–£17,000+ in the Maldives.
Can you island-hop in the Maldives like the Seychelles? ▼
Not easily. Maldives resorts are isolated on individual islands, and transfer between them requires seaplane or speedboat bookings. Some couples do split their stay across two resorts, but logistics are more complex and expensive than Seychelles island-hopping, where regular ferries connect Mahé, Praslin, and La Digue for under £20.
When is the best time to honeymoon in the Maldives vs Seychelles? ▼
The Maldives dry season runs November–April, with peak honeymoon months being December–March. The Seychelles has a slightly different pattern — the calmest, driest weather is April–May and October–November (between the monsoons). Both destinations work year-round, but shoulder seasons offer the best member rates: May for the Maldives, November for the Seychelles.
Which destination is better for snorkelling and diving? ▼
The Maldives wins for accessibility — many resorts have house reefs steps from your villa, and the clear lagoon waters make snorkelling effortless. The Seychelles has excellent diving (especially around Aldabra Atoll) but the waters are deeper and sometimes less clear near the main islands. For beginner snorkellers, the Maldives is more forgiving.
How long should a Maldives vs Seychelles honeymoon be? ▼
Our guest satisfaction data suggests 4–6 nights is optimal for the Maldives (satisfaction dips slightly after 7 nights at a single resort). The Seychelles rewards longer stays — 7–10 nights allows for a split itinerary (e.g., 4 nights Mahé resort + 3 nights Praslin boutique hotel + La Digue day trip) that takes full advantage of the destination's variety.
